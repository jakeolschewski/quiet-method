| Channel | Conservative | Moderate | Aggressive |
|---|---|---|---|
| Organic growth (SEO + word of mouth) | Primary | Primary | Supporting |
| Product Hunt launch | Skipped / minor | One major launch | One major + relaunch |
| Reddit/community seeding | Minimal | Active | Very active |
| Paid acquisition (Google/Meta) | None | None | $500–$2,000/mo |
| CAC (paid, when applicable) | — | — | $8–$15 |
| Influencer/creator deals | None | 3–5 micro | 10+ micro + 2 mid |

### Revenue Mix Model

Each month's revenue = (Monthly subscribers × $4) + (New lifetime deals × $49)

Lifetime deals are front-loaded (higher % in first 3 months due to launch urgency mechanics), then taper.

## Key Model Notes and Caveats

### Free-to-Paid Conversion Rate

Industry benchmarks for productivity Chrome extensions run 1.5%–5% free-to-paid. The moderate scenario at 4% assumes a strong privacy + ADHD angle that drives above-average intent. The aggressive scenario at 6.5% requires exceptional onboarding and a clear moment-of-value in the first week.

**Improve conversion by:**
- Triggering the upgrade email at the moment of first successful intervention (not on a calendar delay)
- Offering lifetime deal within 48 hours of install (scarcity + high intent moment)
- Making the Premium "Weekly Deep Report" preview visible in the free tier (show the insight, blur the detail)

### Churn Rate

An 8% monthly churn in the conservative scenario equals ~65% annual churn — high but realistic for a first-year SaaS with minimal support infrastructure. As the product matures and onboarding improves, target 4%–5% monthly churn by month 12.

**Reduce churn by:**
- Sending the "Your week in calm" report every 7 days without fail (engagement driver)
- Adding a pause option to Premium (vs. cancel) — reduces churn by ~20% in comparable SaaS products
- Win-back sequence for lapsed subscribers at 30, 60, and 90 days post-cancel

### Viral Coefficient

A k-factor of 0.20 (moderate scenario) means every 5 users organically brings in 1 additional user. For a privacy-first productivity tool, word-of-mouth is high because:
- Privacy angle is shareable ("this extension can't spy on you")
- ADHD community shares tools actively within the community
- "Before/after spiral" stories are inherently social

**Improve k-factor by:**
- Adding a "Share your week in calm" feature (shareable weekly stats card)
- Building a simple referral program (free month of Premium for a referral)
- Making the "Guard blocked X spirals this week" stat something users want to brag about

### Lifetime Deal Strategy

Front-loading lifetime deals captures MRR-equivalent revenue early (important for solo founder cash flow) at the cost of LTV. The $49 lifetime deal breaks even vs. subscription at ~12.25 months. Customers who buy lifetime deals have substantially higher advocacy rates than monthly subscribers.

**Lifetime deal recommendation:** Run lifetime aggressively for the first 90 days, then retire it. Reintroduce briefly (48 hours) for anniversary, Black Friday, and major feature launches.

### Key Risk Factors

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chrome Web Store policy change affecting extension permissions | Medium | High | Maintain Firefox port roadmap; avoid over-reliance on any single permission |
| Competitor (e.g., RescueTime) adds similar detection feature | Medium | Medium | Double down on privacy angle and ADHD community — these are hard to copy without architectural commitment |
| Low Product Hunt launch performance | Medium | Medium (Moderate scenario only) | Seed launch timing carefully; have Reddit as backup primary channel |
| Low D30 retention | High (early months) | High | Focus heavily on onboarding improvement; the first successful intervention needs to happen in first session |
| Stripe payment failures / subscriber loss | Low | Medium | Implement Stripe dunning management (automatic retry + reminder emails) |

## Break-Even Analysis

**Monthly fixed costs (estimated):**
- Supabase (Premium sync infrastructure): $25/mo (scales)
- Stripe fees: ~2.9% + $0.30 per transaction (variable)
- Domain + hosting (landing page): $20/mo
- Email platform (ConvertKit or similar): $29/mo
- PostHog analytics (free tier covers first 1M events/mo): $0
- Estimated total fixed: ~$74/mo + Stripe variable

**Conservative scenario break-even:** Month 6–7 (~$650 MRR covers $74 fixed + founder time ROI depends on hourly valuation)

**Moderate scenario break-even:** Month 2 (launch spike revenue covers fixed costs from day one)

**Aggressive scenario:** Never below break-even after month 1 on gross basis; net positive after ad spend from month 2 forward.
